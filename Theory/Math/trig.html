  Θ = angle of right triangle at the origin
  r = hypotenuse
  y = opposite to Θ
  x = adjacent to Θ

       sine  =  sin Θ  =  opposite/hypotenuse  =  y/r
   cosecant  =  csc Θ  =  hypotenuse/opposite  =  r/y  =  1/sin Θ
     cosine  =  cos Θ  =  adjacent/hypotenuse  =  x/r
     secant  =  sec Θ  =  hypotenuse/adjacent  =  r/x  =  1/cos Θ
    tangent  =  tan Θ  =  opposite/adjacent    =  y/x  =  sin Θ/cos θ
  cotangent  =  cot Θ  =  adjacent/opposite    =  x/y  =  1/tan Θ

  (y/r)2 + (x/r)2 = 1
  y2 + x2 = r2

  Pythagorean Theorem: r2 = x2 + y2

Cofunction Identities

  sin Θ = cos (90° - Θ)  ←→  cos Θ = sin (90° - Θ)
  tan Θ = cot (90° - Θ)  ←→  cot Θ = tan (90° - Θ)
  sec Θ = csc (90° - Θ)  ←→  csc Θ = sec (90° - Θ)

Reciprocal Relationships

  csc Θ = 1/sin Θ  ←→  sin Θ = 1/csc Θ
  sec Θ = 1/cos Θ  ←→  cos Θ = 1/sec Θ
  cot Θ = 1/tan Θ  ←→  tan Θ = 1/cot Θ

Pythagorean Theorem

  (length of adjacent)2 + (length of opposite)2 = (length of hypotenuse)2

Pythagorean Theorem for Distance between Points

  d = √((Δx)2 + (Δy)2)


Solving Right Triangles

  1. If you know one of the acute angles, the other will be its complement.
  2. If you know side lengths, use trig functions to find angle.
  3. To find the third side, use the pythagorean theorem or trig functions.

Guidelines for Accuracy
  
  1. Angles to the nearest degree correspond to length to 2 significant digits.
  2. Angles to nearest tenth of a degree (0.1°, 10') correspond to 3 significant digits
  3. Angles to nearest hundredth of a degree (0.01°, 1') correspond to 4 significant digits.
  4. Angles to nearest thousandth of a degree (0.001°, 0.1') correspond to 5 significant digits.

Oblique Triangles
  * Oblique triangles do not contain a right angle.
  * Described and solved mostly with the law of sines and law of cosines, occasionally with the law of tangents.


  The following pertains to a triangle with

          
           ∠Z
         y▵x
        ∠X  z  ∠Y

                  x       y       z
  Law of Sines: ----- = ----- = -----
                sin X   sin Y   sin Z

            
                  x     sin X       y     sin Y       z     sin Z
    therefore:    -  =  -----       -  =  -----       -  =  -----
                  y     sin Y       z     sin Z       x     sin X

                  x     sin X       y     sin Y       z     sin Z
                  -  =  -----       -  =  -----       -  =  -----
                  z     sin Z       x     sin X       y     sin Y


                  x sin Y = y sin X
                  y sin Z = z sin Y
                  z sin X = x sin Z

                  x = y sin X / sin Y
                  x = z sin X / sin Z
                  y = z sin Y / sin Z
                  y = x sin Y / sin X
                  z = x sin Z / sin X
                  z = y sin Z / sin Y

                  sin X = x sin Y / y
                  sin X = x sin Z / z
                  sin Y = y sin Z / z
                  sin Y = y sin X / x
                  sin Z = z sin X / x
                  sin Z = z sin Y / y
                  
  Law of Cosines:

      x2 = y2 + z2 - 2yz cos X
      y2 = z2 + x2 - 2zx cos Y
      z2 = x2 + y2 - 2xy cos Z

    therefore:

      cos X = (y2 + z2 - x2)/2yz
      cos Y = (z2 + x2 - y2)/2zx
      cos Z = (x2 + y2 - z2)/2xy

  Law of Tangents:

      x + y     tan((1/2) * (X+Y))
      -----  =  ------------------
      x - y     tan((1/2) * (X-Y))


Solving Oblique Triangles

  5 Scenarios for Solving Oblique Triangles

    1) SSS Triangle: 3 sides are known
    2) SAS Triangle: 2 sides and 1 angle are known
    3) SSA Triangle: 2 sides and 1 angle are known
    4) ASA Triangle: 1 side and 2 angles are known
    5) AAS Triangle: 1 side and 2 angles are known

  Solving for the 5 Cases

    1) SSS Triangle: 3 sides are known
      * Solving means finding angles X,Y,Z
      * Find the largest angle Z (opposite the largest side) using Law of Cosines
      * Once you know ∠Z, use secant (cos-1) to find ∠Y
      * Use the sum of angles in a triangle to find ∠X
