Chapter 1: Basic Concepts

Newton's Laws of Motion

  1. Body at rest tends to remain at rest or continue to move in a straight line at constant velocity unless acted upon by an external force.
  2. Acceleration of a body is proportional to the resultant force acting on the body, and this acceleration is in the same direction as the resultant force. Written as F(orce) = m(ass) * a(cceleration)
  3. For every force acting on a body (action) there is an equal and opposite reacting force (reaction), where the reaction is collinear to the acting force.

Coordinate System

Book uses a standard, right-handed Cartesian coordinate system, in which rotations are measured positive counterclockwise.

Vectors

  • Vector is a quantity with magnitude and direction.
  • Indicated with boldface for the whole vector, standard type for the magnitude only, possibly separated by coordinate axis components. F, F, Fx
  • Asterisk indicated vector dot product
  • Caret indicates vector cross product

Derivatives and Integrals

  • Derivative is the rate of change in one variable WRT another variable.
  • Integrals are the reverse/inverse, represented by symbol ∫
