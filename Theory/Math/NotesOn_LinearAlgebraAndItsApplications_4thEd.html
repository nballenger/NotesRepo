Chapter 1: Matrices and Gaussian Elimination

1.1 Introduction

  • Central problem of linear algebra is solving linear equations.
  • Most important/simplest case is a system of n equations in n unknowns.
  • Most efficient algorithm for solving systems of equations of arbitrary size is Gaussian Elimination.
  • In addition to that algorithm, four deeper aspects addressed in the chapter:
    • The geometry of planes - systems of linear equations create (typically) intersecting planes in whatever dimensional space they cover.
    • Matrix notation, transpose matrices, and inverse matrices
    • Exceptional cases (no solution or infinite solutions)
    • Algorithmic cost (steps) to perform elimination in a system of size n
